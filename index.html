About

Hello! I am a research scholar in the Lakkaraju Lab at UCSF Ophthalmology, supported by the National Eye Institute through the PROPEL postbaccalaureate program. I will be starting my Ph.D. at Columbia University in the Disease & Therapeutics track of Vagelos College this fall.

I am broadly interested in retinal pigment epithelium (RPE) biology, lysosomal quality control, and mTOR signaling, with a particular focus on how these pathways fail in age-related macular degeneration (AMD) and related neurodegenerative disease. More broadly, I want to understand why post-mitotic tissues — retina and brain — fail late, and what genetic and cellular modifiers determine resilience.

Previously, I was an undergraduate in the lab of Dr. James R. Carey, where I first learned how to ask questions of a dataset. I came to research through a long detour from premed; somewhere in that detour I realized that finding a real gap in the literature — and having the tools to fill it — is the closest thing to a superpower I'll ever have.
